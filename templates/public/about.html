About Raahi

From the heart of Team Ghost, a vision for safer cities

Our Mission

Raahi means "traveler" in Hindi, and our mission is to ensure every traveler can navigate urban spaces with confidence and security. We believe that everyone deserves to feel safe in their community.

Using cutting-edge AI technology, we're creating a platform that detects potential safety threats in real-time, providing citizens with the information they need to make informed decisions about their routes and travel plans.

Our goal is not to create fear, but to empower communities with knowledge and tools that enhance public safety and emergency response.

The Technology Behind Raahi

AI Detection

Our system uses YOLOv8, a state-of-the-art computer vision model that can detect multiple objects in real-time. We've trained it to recognize emergency situations such as fires, physical altercations, and dangerous conditions.

Audio Analysis

Raahi can detect emergency keywords in audio streams, such as calls for help, providing an additional layer of safety monitoring.

Safety Mapping

Areas are classified by safety level based on incident history, creating a visual heatmap that helps users identify safer routes through urban areas.

Emergency Response

Our SOS feature allows users to quickly signal for help in emergency situations, automatically marking their location and alerting administrators.

Frequently Asked Questions

Our AI models are trained on diverse datasets and are continuously improved. While no AI system is perfect, we typically achieve 85-95% accuracy in detecting common safety incidents. Each detection includes a confidence score, and all high-impact alerts are verified by human administrators before triggering emergency responses.

Raahi is designed with privacy in mind. We only collect location data when explicitly provided by users (such as during an SOS alert). Our system does not track individual users, and all public safety data is aggregated and anonymized. We never share personal information with third parties without consent.

If you witness an emergency situation, your first action should always be to contact local emergency services (police, fire, ambulance). Raahi is designed to complement these services, not replace them. After contacting authorities, you can use the SOS feature to mark the location for other users' awareness.

We welcome community involvement! You can contribute by providing feedback on the platform, reporting any issues you encounter, or volunteering for our testing programs. For technical contributors, our platform is built with open technologies, and we're always looking for developers passionate about public safety.
